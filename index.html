About


  • Juan Shishido
  • UC Berkeley
  • School of Information

Juan is motivated by the belief that anyone can learn to code and can use data to make better decisions. His goal is to empower everyone to do so by demystifying the process and showing what is possible. He has been inspired by countless individuals and wants to do the same for others. Juan believes in open data, open source, and open knowledge.

Currently a masters student at the School of Information, Juan is exploring various facets of data science. He is focusing on machine learning, information visualization, and natural language processing to augment his data analysis experience. He is inspired by companies that use data to inform product decisions that positively affect user experience. Juan is a Data Analyst Intern at DataScience, a Los Angeles-based startup that analyzes data to deliver actionable insights to companies. He is also a graduate student researcher at the D-Lab, a campus resource enabling data intensive social science, and a part of the Data Science Collaborative, a group working to develop innovative data science solutions to challenges in academia, government, non-profit, and industry.

Prior to graduate school, Juan worked as an evaluator, where he assessed program impacts and established causality using regression models and algorithm-based methods. He constructed matching algorithms to select non-participants with observable characteristics similar to program participants. He also created customer-specific energy analytics, comparing each customer’s cost and consumption to similar households identified using hierarchical clustering. Juan has designed and selected samples, both simple and stratified, and has experience with entity resolution methodologies.

Juan primarily uses Python, but also has experience with R, SAS, and Stata.
