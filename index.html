About

Juan is motivated by the belief that anyone can learn to code and can use data to make better decisions. His goal is to empower everyone to do so by demystifying the process and showing what is possible. He has been inspired by countless individuals and wants to do the same for others. Juan believes in open data, open source, and open knowledge.

Currently a masters student at the School of Information, Juan is exploring various facets of data science. He is focusing on machine learning, information visualization, and natural language processing to augment his data analysis experience. Juan is a graduate student researcher at the D-Lab, a campus resource enabling data intensive social science, and also contributes to Localwise, a Berkeley startup out of Skydeck, the university's startup accelerator.

Prior to graduate school, Juan worked as an evaluator, where he assessed program impacts and established causality using regression models and algorithm-based methods. He constructed matching algorithms to select non-participants with observable characteristics similar to program participants. He also created customer-specific energy analytics, comparing each customerâ€™s cost and consumption to similar households identified using hierarchical clustering. Juan has designed and selected samples, both simple and stratified, and has experience with entity resolution methodologies.

Juan primarily uses Python, but also has experience with R, SAS, and Stata.
